Paperweight Design Language · v1

Brick Mode

Put it down.

An app that turns your phone into a paperweight — so the quiet, the forest, and your own attention can come back.

The feeling

A heavy, calm object on a still desk.

01 — Stillness

Nothing pulses for attention. No badges, no streaks shouting. The app is quiet on purpose.

02 — Weight

Choosing to disconnect should feel solid and intentional — like setting something down, not flicking a switch.

03 — Living

The reward for absence is something growing — a forest that fills in while you're away.

04 — Black

True OLED black is the canvas. Pixels that aren't lit are pixels at rest.

Color

True black, warm green.

The greens lean warm — moss, olive, dawn-light on leaves — never clinical teal. They glow against the black like the only lit thing in a dark room.

Illustration

The forest fills in while you're gone.

Flat, geometric, layered — a calm Kurzgesagt-style world. Simple shapes, warm-green gradients, a single glowing light, and a few fireflies for life. It grows denser the longer a session runs.

Two Directions

Same soul, two ways to hold it.

Both share the OLED-black canvas, the warm greens, and the poetic voice. They differ in form: one is glass and air, the other is moss and ground.

Voice & Tone

Calm. Poetic. Never preachy.

“Put it down. The world keeps turning without your thumb.”

“Nothing here needs you right now. That's the gift.”

“Two hours of quiet. The forest noticed.”

“Heavy, still, and yours. Welcome back to slow.”

We do

Speak softly. Use nature. Reward absence. Trust the user already knows why they're here.

We don't

Shame, nag, gamify with streaks-as-pressure, or shout in notifications. No fear, no FOMO inverted.
